The IBM founder Mr. Thomas J. Watson Sr.

Thomas J. Watson Sr. was one of the most successful and famous entrepreneurs in the first half of the 20th century.
With a management style that was unique at the time, he made IBM the leading supplier of punched card systems in the world.

Before becoming President of IBM, Thomas J. Watson Jr.'s determination did not equal his father's. His performance at school
was poor, and he was only interested in flying. World War II, in which he served as a pilot in the U.S. Air Force, changed
his life. He became resolute in his aim to succeed his father at IBM and to prepare the company for the computer age that was
just beginning. He asserted himself against his father, and promoted the development of electronic punched card technology
and data processing. Whereas Watson Sr. stands for IBM's success as a punched card company, Watson Jr. stands for
its success in computer technology.
